**Degree computation for a directed, unweighted graph:**
For a given node (v), the degree measures how many edges are incident to (v). In a directed graph, we distinguish between **out-degree** (the number of directed edges leaving (v)) and **in-degree** (the number of directed edges entering (v)). The out-degree of (v) is defined as the number of nodes (u) such that there is a directed edge ((v,u)), while the in-degree of (v) is defined as the number of nodes (u) such that there is a directed edge ((u,v)).

---

**Example 1: A small directed graph**

Graph: nodes ({A, B, C, D}), directed edges: ((A,B), (A,C), (C,A), (D,A))
Task: compute the in-degree and out-degree of node (A)

**Step 1:** List all outgoing edges of (A).
From the edge list, (A) has outgoing edges ((A,B)) and ((A,C)).

**Step 2:** Count the outgoing edges of (A).
There are 2 outgoing edges, so the out-degree of (A) is 2.

**Step 3:** List all incoming edges of (A).
From the edge list, (A) has incoming edges ((C,A)) and ((D,A)).

**Step 4:** Count the incoming edges of (A).
There are 2 incoming edges, so the in-degree of (A) is 2.

**Step 5:** (Optional) Compute the total degree of (A).
The total degree of (A) is the sum of its in-degree and out-degree, which is (2 + 2 = 4).
