**Degree computation for an undirected, unweighted graph:**
For a given node (v), the degree measures how many edges are incident to (v). In an undirected graph, an edge ({v,u}) contributes equally to both endpoints, so the degree of (v) is simply the number of distinct neighbors directly connected to (v). If self-loops are allowed, each self-loop contributes 2 to the degree; otherwise, each incident edge contributes 1.

---

**Example 1: A simple undirected graph**

Graph: nodes ({A, B, C, D}), undirected edges: ({A,B}, {A,C}, {C,D})
Task: compute the degree of node (A)

**Step 1:** List all edges incident to (A).
From the edge list, (A) is connected by edges ({A,B}) and ({A,C}).

**Step 2:** Identify the neighbors of (A).
The neighbors of (A) are the nodes directly connected to it: ({B, C}).

**Step 3:** Count the neighbors (or incident edges).
There are 2 neighbors of (A), so there are 2 edges incident to (A).

**Step 4:** Define the degree of (A).
The degree of node (A) is the number of incident edges, which is 2.
