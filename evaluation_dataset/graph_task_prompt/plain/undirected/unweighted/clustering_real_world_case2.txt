Real-world task: Model a city’s street network as an undirected graph where nodes are intersections and edges are road segments, then compute the clustering coefficient for each intersection and the overall average. This measures how often an intersection’s neighboring intersections are directly connected to one another (forming small loops), revealing fine-grained street meshes, local redundancy, and alternative route availability—useful for assessing walkability, traffic robustness, and distinguishing grid-like areas from cul-de-sac–heavy neighborhoods.
