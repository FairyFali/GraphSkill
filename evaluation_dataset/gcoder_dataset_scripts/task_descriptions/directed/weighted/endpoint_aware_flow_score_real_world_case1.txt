Compute an endpoint-aware patient-transfer score for a healthcare referral network between a specified referring facility, _referrer, and a receiving facility, _receiver. This score multiplies the maximum daily transfer volume by a factor that reflects how well-connected the neighborhoods of the endpoints are within the broader collaboration network.

The input is a directed network of referral pathways with finite, nonnegative real capacities on edges, represented by triples (u, v, w) where w is the capacity of referrals from u to v (e.g., patients per day); if multiple triples share the same ordered pair (u, v), their capacities are summed. Let F(_referrer, _receiver) be the maximum feasible transfer volume from _referrer to _receiver respecting these capacities. Construct an undirected simple collaboration graph on the same facilities by replacing every directed referral (u, v) with a single undirected link {u, v} (if both directions exist, keep only one; ignore capacities). For any facility x, let C(x) be its local clustering coefficient in this undirected graph, defined as the fraction of unordered neighbor pairs of x that are directly linked (and 0 if x has fewer than two neighbors). Define S = F(_referrer, _receiver) * (1 + (C(_referrer) + C(_receiver)) / 2).

Return S as a real-valued number.
