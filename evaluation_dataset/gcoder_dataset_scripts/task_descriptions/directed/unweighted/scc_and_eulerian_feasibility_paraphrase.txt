You are given a directed, unweighted graph and must compute a structural complexity score that depends on the existence of an Eulerian path and on the graph’s strongly connected components. The input is an edge list: a sequence of ordered pairs (u, v), each denoting a directed edge from u to v. An Eulerian path is a directed trail that traverses every edge exactly once. A strongly connected component (SCC) is a maximal group of nodes where, for any two nodes a and b in the group, there is a directed path from a to b and from b to a. If the graph has an Eulerian path, the score is the size (number of nodes) of the largest SCC; otherwise, the score is the total count of SCCs in the graph. Return this structural complexity score as an integer.
