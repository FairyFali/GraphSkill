You are auditing a website’s navigation structure and must compute a usability complexity score that depends on whether the site allows a single click-through session and on its mutually navigable page clusters. The input is an edge list: a sequence of ordered pairs (u, v), each representing a hyperlink from page u to page v. A browsing session that clicks every hyperlink exactly once is an Eulerian path. A strongly connected component (SCC) is a maximal group of pages such that for every pair of pages (a, b) in the group, there exists a path of links from a to b and from b to a. If the site has an Eulerian path, the score is the size (number of pages) of the largest SCC; otherwise, the score is the total number of SCCs in the site. Return this usability complexity score as an integer.
