In a weighted undirected graph $G$, we are interested in how “tightly” two nodes $s$ and $t$ are coupled, taking into account both their shared neighborhood and their positions in the graph.

For nodes $s$ and $t$, define:

* $d(s,t)$: the minimum total edge weight required to travel between $s$ and $t$,
* $N_{\text{common}}$: the nodes that share an edge with both $s$ and $t$, with $|N_{\text{common}}|$ its size,
* $C(s)$ and $C(t)$: the neighborhood interconnection values of $s$ and $t$, computed on the unweighted version of 
$G$, respectively.

The **pair tightness score** is then
$$
T(s,t) = \frac{|N_{\text{common}}| + \frac{C(s) + C(t)}{2}}{1 + d(s,t)}.
$$

**Task.**
Given a weighted undirected graph $G$ and two specified nodes $s$ and $t$, evaluate the pair tightness score $T(s,t)$ as defined above and return its value.
