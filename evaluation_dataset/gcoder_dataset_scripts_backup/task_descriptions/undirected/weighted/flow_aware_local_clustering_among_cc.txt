Consider a weighted undirected graph $G$ with a distinguished **sink** node $t$.
For each a maximal group $C$ of nodes that can all reach one another in $G$, select a canonical **representative source** node $s_C$ in $C$ (for example, the node with the smallest ID in that component).

For every such subset $C$, define its **component score** relative to $t$ as
$$
S_C = F(s_C, t) \cdot \bigl(1 + L(s_C)\bigr),
$$
where:

* $ F(s_C, t) $ denotes the greatest achievable transfer value from $ s_C $ to $ t $ under the edge-capacity constraints of the graph, and
* $ L(s_C) $ is a quantity that measures how strongly the immediate neighbors of $ s_C $ are interconnected when edge directions are ignored.

**Task.**
Given the graph $ G $ and the designated node $ t $, identify the node $ s $ whose associated component attains the largest value of $ S_C $, and return this node.
