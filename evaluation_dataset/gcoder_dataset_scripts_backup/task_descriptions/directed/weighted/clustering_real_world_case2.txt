"Imagine an interbank lending network where each node is a bank and each directed edge from A to B is weighted by the size of Bank A’s exposure to Bank B (for example, the total amount loaned). In this directed, weighted network, compute the local and overall clustering coefficients to assess how often a bank’s counterparties are also directly connected to one another, and how strong those links are (forming heavily weighted triangles). This highlights tightly interlinked lending circles and clusters of banks where dense, high-exposure triads may amplify contagion risk if one institution in the group experiences distress."
