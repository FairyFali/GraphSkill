Imagine you are analysing a small directed social network, where each node is a person and a directed edge from A to B means “A follows B” on a platform like Twitter. For a particular user v, you want to understand how tightly knit their local community is: do the people that v follows also tend to follow each other, forming a close, cluster-like neighbourhood, or are they mostly disconnected from one another? Given this follower graph, your task is to focus on user v and compute their clustering coefficient, a value that quantifies how strongly v’s neighbours are interconnected based on the directed edges between them. This score will help you judge whether v sits in a tightly clustered community (many mutual follow relationships among their neighbours) or a more loosely connected, scattered neighbourhood.
