Imagine an interbank lending network where each node is a bank and a directed edge from A to B means "Bank A has extended a credit line to Bank B." . Compute the local and overall clustering coefficients in this directed network to assess how often a bank’s counterparties (the banks it lends to or borrows from) are also directly connected to one another, forming tightly linked triads of lending relationships. This reveals tightly interwoven lending circles and highlights clusters of banks where lending ties are densely interconnected, potentially amplifying contagion risk if one institution in the cluster comes under stress.
