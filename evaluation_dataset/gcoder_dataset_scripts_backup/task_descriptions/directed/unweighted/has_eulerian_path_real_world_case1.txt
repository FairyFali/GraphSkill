Real‑world paraphrase: Given a map of one‑way streets (intersections are vertices, streets are directed edges), plan a route for a vehicle (e.g., a street sweeper or mail carrier) that traverses every street exactly once. Such a route exists only if, when you ignore direction, the network is connected on all intersections that touch a street, and the in/out flow at intersections is balanced: either every intersection has equal in‑degree and out‑degree (a closed tour), or exactly two intersections are imbalanced—one has one more outgoing than incoming street (start), and one has one more incoming than outgoing street (end).
